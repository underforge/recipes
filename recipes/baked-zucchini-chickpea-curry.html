Baked Zucchini Chickpea Curry

Prep: 15 min Cook: 30 min Serves: 4 Vegan · GF · Soy-free
Original recipe: Vegan Richa ↗

My Changes

Aaron's Modifications

  • Omitted hot sauce and sambal oelek — substituted Sweet Chili Sauce instead
  • Added broccoli and cauliflower in similar quantities to the zucchini
  • Used garlic powder instead of fresh garlic / ginger garlic paste

Ingredients

  • Vegetables
  • 2 tspoil (for greasing)
  • 1 cuponion, chopped
  • 1 mediumzucchini, sliced ¼" or cubed
  • similar qtybroccoli florets ✱ added
  • similar qtycauliflower florets ✱ added
  • Heat / Sauce
  • to tasteSweet Chili Sauce ✱ replaces hot sauce + sambal oelek
  • Spices
  • 2 tspground coriander
  • 1 tspground cumin
  • 1–2 tspgaram masala
  • ¼ tspsalt
  • Curry Base
  • to tastegarlic powder ✱ replaces ginger garlic paste
  • 8 ozcanned tomato puree (or 2 tbsp tomato paste)
  • 15 ozfull-fat coconut milk
  • ½ tspsalt
  • 15 oz canchickpeas, drained
  • ½ cupfrozen spinach (or more, to taste)
  • Garnish
  • cilantro and lime juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C). Grease a 9×12" baking dish with oil. Add onion, zucchini, broccoli, cauliflower, and sweet chili sauce, tossing well.
  2. Mix all spices in a small bowl and sprinkle over the veggie mixture. Spread evenly in the pan. Bake for 10–14 minutes until onions are translucent.
  3. Remove from oven. Mix in garlic powder and tomato puree. Add coconut milk, chickpeas, and spinach. Stir well and spread evenly.
  4. Return to oven and bake 20–30 minutes, until sauce is bubbling and slightly thickened. If you need it thicker, stir in a few tablespoons of non-dairy yogurt while still hot.
  5. Garnish with cilantro and lime juice. Serve with rice, naan, flatbread, or garlic bread.
