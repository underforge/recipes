Fresh Peach Cobbler

Prep: 30 min Cook: 45 min Serves: 10–12 Vegetarian · Nut-free
Original recipe: Sally's Baking Addiction ↗

Ingredients

  • Peach Filling
  • 10 cups (~1.8kg)fresh peaches, peeled and chopped into 1–1½" chunks
  • ¼ cup (50g)packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1 Tbsp (7g)cornstarch
  • 1 Tbspfresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsppure vanilla extract
  • ½ tspground cinnamon
  • ⅛ tspground nutmeg
  • ⅛ tspground ginger
  • ⅛ tspsalt
  • Biscuit Topping
  • 2 cups (250g)all-purpose flour, spooned and leveled
  • ½ cup (100g)granulated sugar
  • 1½ tspbaking powder
  • ¼ tspbaking soda
  • ½ tspsalt
  • ½ cup (113g)unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ½ cup (120ml)buttermilk, cold
  • Assembly
  • 1 largeegg beaten with 1 Tbsp milk or buttermilk (egg wash)
  • 2 Tbsp (optional)granulated sugar mixed with ½ tsp cinnamon, for topping

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C). Lightly grease a 9×13" baking pan or any 3- to 4-quart baking dish.
  2. Combine all filling ingredients in a large bowl, then spread evenly into the pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then remove and set aside. Keep the oven on.
  3. Whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Cut in cold butter using a pastry cutter or two forks until the mixture resembles coarse, pea-sized crumbles. Pour in buttermilk and gently mix until just combined. Dough should be slightly sticky; add 1 extra tablespoon buttermilk if too dry.
  4. Take handfuls of dough, gently flatten into patties, and lay them across the warm peach filling, covering most of the fruit.
  5. Brush biscuit topping with egg wash, then sprinkle with cinnamon-sugar if desired.
  6. Bake 40–50 minutes until the topping is golden brown and the peach filling is bubbling around the edges. A toothpick inserted into the biscuit should come out clean.
  7. Cool on a rack for at least 5 minutes before serving warm. Great with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream. Store leftovers covered in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
