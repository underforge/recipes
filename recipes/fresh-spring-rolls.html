Fresh Spring Rolls with Peanut Sauce

Prep: 35 min Serves: 2–4 Vegan · GF · Soy-free
Original recipe: Love and Lemons ↗

My Changes

Aaron's Modifications

  • Fake Soy Sauce in place of tamari/soy sauce in the peanut sauce

Ingredients

  • Peanut Sauce
  • ½ cupcreamy natural peanut butter
  • 2 Tbspfresh lime juice
  • 2 Tbsprice vinegar
  • 1 Tbspsriracha
  • 1 TbspFake Soy Sauce ✱ replaces tamari/soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsptoasted sesame oil
  • 2 tspmaple syrup
  • 1 tspfresh ginger, grated
  • 4–6 Tbspwater, as needed
  • Spring Rolls
  • 4 ozvermicelli rice noodles
  • 8 largerice paper wrappers
  • 1carrot, julienned
  • ½English cucumber, julienned
  • 1jalapeño, seeded and cut into strips
  • 1mango, peeled and cut into strips
  • 1avocado, cut into strips
  • handfulfresh basil, mint, and cilantro leaves

Instructions

  1. Make the peanut sauce: whisk together the peanut butter, lime juice, rice vinegar, sriracha, Fake Soy Sauce, sesame oil, maple syrup, ginger, and 4 Tbsp water until smooth. Add more water 1 Tbsp at a time until you reach your preferred dipping consistency. Taste and adjust seasoning. Set aside.
  2. Cook the noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse well under cold water.
  3. Fill a large bowl with warm water and dampen a clean kitchen towel. Spread the towel flat over your work surface — this is where you'll assemble the rolls.
  4. Soak one rice paper wrapper in the warm water for about 15 seconds until pliable but not floppy. Lay it flat on the towel. Arrange a small amount of noodles, carrot, cucumber, jalapeño, mango, avocado, and fresh herbs across the centre.
  5. Fold the left and right sides of the wrapper inward over the filling, then roll from the bottom up, pulling firmly to keep it tight. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.
  6. Serve immediately with peanut sauce for dipping. Leftover sauce keeps in the fridge for up to 5 days — thin with a splash of water before serving if it thickens.
