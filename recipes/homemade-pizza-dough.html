Homemade Pizza Dough

Prep: 135 min Cook: 15 min Serves: 2 12-inch pizzas Vegetarian
Original recipe: Sally's Baking Addiction ↗

Ingredients

  • Dough
  • 1⅓ cups (320ml)warm water (about 110°F / 43°C)
  • 1 Tbsp (13g)granulated sugar
  • 2¼ tsp (7g)instant yeast (1 standard packet)
  • 3½ to 4 cups (438–500g)all-purpose flour, plus more as needed
  • 2 Tbsp (30ml)extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for greasing
  • 1 tspsalt
  • sprinklecornmeal, for dusting the pan
  • Toppings (per pizza)
  • extra-virgin olive oil, for brushing
  • 1 clovegarlic, minced (optional)
  • ½ cup (125g)pizza sauce
  • 2 cups (227g)shredded mozzarella

Instructions

  1. Whisk the warm water, sugar, and yeast together in the bowl of your stand mixer fitted with a dough hook. Cover and let rest for 5 minutes until foamy. (No stand mixer? Use a large bowl and mix by hand with a wooden spoon.)
  2. Add the flour, olive oil, and salt. Beat on medium speed for 2 minutes until the dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl.
  3. Knead: keep the dough in the mixer and beat for 5 full minutes, or knead by hand on a lightly floured surface for 5 minutes. Add flour 1 tsp at a time only if the dough is too sticky to handle — don't overdo it. The dough is ready when it passes the windowpane test: stretch a golf ball–sized piece until it's thin enough for light to pass through without tearing.
  4. Rise: place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, turning to coat. Cover with plastic wrap or a towel and let rise at room temperature for 60–90 minutes until doubled in size. (On a cold day: heat your oven to 150°F / 66°C, turn it off, then put the covered dough inside with the door slightly ajar.)
  5. Preheat oven to 475°F (246°C) and let it heat for at least 15–20 minutes while you shape the pizza. If using a pizza stone, preheat it in the oven too. Lightly grease a baking sheet or pizza pan and sprinkle with cornmeal.
  6. Shape: punch down the dough to release air bubbles, then divide in half. On a lightly floured surface, flatten each piece into a disc, then stretch and press into a 12-inch circle about ½-inch thick. If the dough keeps springing back, cover it and rest for 5–10 minutes before trying again. Pinch the edges up to form a rim.
  7. Cover the shaped dough loosely and rest for a few minutes while you prep your toppings.
  8. Press dents into the surface with your fingers to prevent bubbling. Brush with olive oil, scatter garlic if using, spread ½ cup pizza sauce, and top with 2 cups shredded mozzarella plus any other toppings. Bake for 13–15 minutes until the crust is deep golden brown.

Variations

Double Batch (4 pizzas) — adjusted yeast

  • Double all ingredients except the yeast
  • Yeast: use 3 tsp (9g) instead of 4½ tsp (14g). This is about 1.3× rather than 2×
  • Why: yeast reproduces as it ferments, so doubling it causes the dough to rise too fast, which leaves less time for flavour development and can leave a yeasty aftertaste. At 9g the dough takes the same baker's-percentage yeast as a slow Neapolitan-style dough (~1%), giving you a slightly longer rise (allow 75–90 min) but noticeably better flavour
  • Everything else stays the same — same kneading time, same shaping, same bake
